### Crisis + Substance Use Treatment Referral
**Call SAMHSA's National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357)**
- Hours: 24/7/365
- Languages: English, Spanish
- Services: Treatment referrals, recovery support, mental health questions
- Cost: Free and confidential
- TTY: 1-800-487-4889

**What It Provides:**
Free information and referrals to local treatment facilities, support groups, and community resources for substance use and mental health disorders.

---

### Major Recovery Fellowships (All Free, Anonymous, No Registration)

#### **Alcoholics Anonymous (AA)**
- **What:** Peer-support fellowship for alcohol recovery (12-step program)
- **Meetings:** In-person + online, daily/weekly
- **Find:** https://www.aa.org/meetings
- **Cost:** Free (donations optional)
- **Participation:** Open to anyone with desire to stop drinking

#### **Narcotics Anonymous (NA)**
- **What:** Peer-support fellowship for any and all substance/addiction recovery (12-step program)
- **Meetings:** In-person + online, daily/weekly
- **Find:** https://na.org/meetingsearch
- **Cost:** Free (donations optional)
- **Participation:** Open to anyone seeking recovery from addiction

#### **Recovery Dharma**
- **What:** Buddhist-inspired recovery community (meditation, Four Noble Truths, peer support)
- **Meetings:** In-person + online, daily/weekly, worldwide
- **Find:** https://recoverydharma.org/locations
- **Cost:** Free
- **Participation:** Secular; no religious requirement; beginner-friendly
- **Unique:** Grounded in Buddhist philosophy; strong emphasis on community (sangha)

#### **SMART Recovery**
- **What:** Evidence-based, secular recovery using CBT and motivational interviewing
- **Meetings:** In-person + online, daily/weekly
- **Find:** https://smartrecovery.org/meeting
- **Cost:** Free (donations optional)
- **Participation:** No 12 steps; self-empowerment focus; no religious/spiritual requirement
- **Unique:** Science-based; includes trained facilitators

#### **Buddhist Recovery Network**
- **What:** Meta-network of Buddhist recovery programs (Recovery Dharma, Refuge Recovery, RAFT to Freedom, Heart of Recovery, etc.)
- **Find:** https://buddhistrecovery.org
- **Beginner Meetings:** https://buddhistrecovery.org/recover/beginner-friendly-meetings
- **Cost:** Free
- **Participation:** Secular; welcoming to all

## ğŸ‘¥ TIER 3: SPECIALIZED POPULATIONS

### Veterans & Service Members

**Veterans Crisis Line**
- **Call:** 988, then Press 1 (easiest) OR 1-800-273-8255, then Press 1 (legacy)
- **Text:** 838255
- **Chat:** https://www.veteranscrisisline.net/Chat
- **Hours:** 24/7/365
- **What:** Crisis support for veterans, service members, families, caregivers
- **Unique:** VCL responders many are veterans themselves; understand military culture, PTSD, transition

**Homeless Veterans Support**
- **Call:** 1-877-4AID-VET (1-877-424-3838)
- **Hours:** 24/7/365
- **What:** Free information on VA housing, employment, healthcare, mental health services for homeless/at-risk veterans

**VA Mental Health & Substance Use Services**
- Website: https://www.va.gov/health/
- Phone: https://www.va.gov/health/mentalhealth/ (local VA medical center numbers)

### Youth & Runaway

**National Runaway Safeline**
- **Call:** 1-800-RUNAWAY (1-800-786-2929)
- **Text:** Send "HELLO" to 66008
- **Chat:** https://www.1800runaway.org
- **Hours:** 24/7/365
- **Who:** Youth under 18; young adults in crisis; parents seeking help
- **Services:** Crisis counseling, local resource referral, can assist with safe housing

### Homelessness & Housing

**Dial 211 (Universal Community Resources)**
- **Call:** 211
- **Web:** https://www.211.org
- **Hours:** 24/7/365
- **Languages:** English, Spanish, and others
- **Services:** Emergency shelter, food, housing assistance, healthcare, substance use treatment, utilities, job training, domestic violence services
- **Available:** All 50 US states

**Domestic Violence Support**
- **National Domestic Violence Hotline:** 1-800-799-7233
- **Chat:** thehotline.org
- **Text:** 88788
- **Hours:** 24/7/365

## âœ¨ DESIGN PRINCIPLES FOR YOUR SCREEN

### 1. **Crisis First, Always**
- Place 988 and 911 at top, always visible
- Use visual hierarchy (large buttons, prominent color)

### 2. **Multimodal Access**
- For each resource: show ALL options (call, text, chat, link)
- Some users may not be able to call (in unsafe situation)

### 3. **Plain Language**
- Use "suicide" not "suicidal ideation"
- Use "feeling overwhelmed" not "acute distress"
- Avoid clinical jargon

### 4. **Transparency About App**
- Clear disclaimer: "Not monitored," "Not a substitute"
- Emphasis on privacy: Recovery & Renewal doesn't track external resource use

### 5. **Accessibility**
- Large tap targets (44Ã—44 pixels minimum)
- High contrast text (4.5:1 minimum)
- Clickable phone numbers (tap to call)
- Copyable text numbers (for texting)
- Works offline (phone numbers as plain text, not just links)
- Mobile-optimized, low-bandwidth compatible

### 6. **Inclusive Language**
- "You and your loved ones"
- Include options for supporting someone else
- Acknowledge diverse identities (LGBTQ+, veterans, etc.)

### 7. **Link Directly to Official Resources**
- Always link to official sites (988lifeline.org, aa.org, recoverydharma.org)
- Do NOT embed or cache meeting schedules
- Respect fellowship guidelines

### 8. **Emphasize Self-Direction & Anonymity**
- "You decide when to reach out"
- "You don't have to give your name"
- "All calls are free and confidential"
