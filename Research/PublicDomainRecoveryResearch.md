## SECTION 3: ACADEMIC RESEARCH ON MEDITATION & RECOVERY

### 3.1 KEY FINDINGS: MEDITATION IS EFFECTIVE FOR ADDICTION RECOVERY

#### **Meta-Analysis Evidence (2025-2023)**
- **Mindfulness-Oriented Recovery Enhancement (MORE):** 
  - Meta-analysis (2022, 9+ RCTs) showed significant effects on addiction behaviors, psychiatric distress, chronic pain
  - Effect sizes: Large for addiction outcomes

- **Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention (MBRP):**
  - Systematic review (2017, 15+ RCTs): Reduces substance use, cravings, relapse risk
  - Improves emotional regulation
  - Long-term effects at 12-month follow-up

- **Spiritual Meditation on Addiction Recovery:**
  - 2020 systematic review & meta-analysis (14 studies)
  - Findings: Reduces addiction behavior, depression, anxiety, stress
  - Improves psychological and mental health outcomes

#### **Key Mechanisms Identified:**
1. **Prefrontal Cortex Activation** - Enhanced self-control, decision-making
2. **Amygdala Regulation** - Reduced emotional reactivity, craving intensity
3. **Craving Reduction** - 20-40% reductions in substance craving in studies
4. **Emotional Regulation** - Improved ability to sit with discomfort
5. **Reward Re-calibration** - Restores salience to natural rewards (food, connection, achievement)

#### **Optimal Practice Parameters (from research):**
- **Frequency Matters More Than Duration:** 4-7 days/week more important than longer sessions
- **Regular Practice Key:** Consistency > intensity
- **Session Length:** 10-20 minutes optimal for adherence; longer sessions don't necessarily improve outcomes
- **Variety of Practices:** Mixing interoceptive (body focus) and exteroceptive (external focus) meditations most effective
